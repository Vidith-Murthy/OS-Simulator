About SJF (Non-Preemptive)

Algorithm overview, characteristics and trade-offs

Shortest Job First (SJF) selects the process with the smallest execution time next. In non-preemptive mode, once a process begins executing it runs to completion, regardless of shorter processes that arrive later.

Key Characteristics

  • Non-preemptive: once started, runs to completion
  • Processes execute in order of burst time (shortest first)
  • Requires knowledge of burst time in advance
  • Theoretically optimal for minimizing average waiting time

Advantages

  • Minimizes average waiting time compared to FCFS
  • Gives preference to short processes, improving throughput
  • Reduces the "convoy effect"
  • Better response time for short processes

Disadvantages

  • Can cause starvation for longer processes
  • Requires advance knowledge of burst time (rarely available)
  • Not ideal for interactive systems
  • No priority consideration for time-sensitive tasks
